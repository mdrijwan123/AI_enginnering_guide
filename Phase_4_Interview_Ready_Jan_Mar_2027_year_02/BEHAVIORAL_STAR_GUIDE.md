## 📐 STAR Framework — The Template

```
S — Situation:  Set the scene. What was the context? 2–3 sentences max.
T — Task:       What was YOUR specific responsibility? Be explicit.
A — Action:     What did YOU do? (Not "we") — specific steps, decisions, tradeoffs.
R — Result:     Quantified outcome. What changed? What was the impact? What did you learn?
```

**Timings:** Each story should take 2–3 minutes to tell (don't rush, don't ramble).

**Anti-patterns:**
- ❌ Saying "we" too much — interviewers want to know YOUR contribution
- ❌ Vague outcomes ("it was better") — always quantify
- ❌ Technical ramblings — STAR is about behavior, not implementation
- ❌ Stories without conflict/difficulty — the best stories have genuine challenges

## 🎙️ Delivery Tips

**Structuring your verbal answer:**
1. Brief header: "Sure — a good example of this is when I was at [company] working on [context]..."
2. Story in STAR order: 30s situation → 15s task → 90s action → 30s result
3. Reflection: "Looking back, what I learned was..." or "I'd do X differently because..."
4. Stop. Don't ramble. Let the interviewer ask a follow-up.

**Language patterns that signal maturity:**
- "I specifically owned..." (clear accountability)
- "The trade-off I was weighing was..." (structured thinking)
- "The data showed..." (evidence-based)
- "In hindsight, I would..." (self-awareness)

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Blaming others for failures
- Describing team success without your specific contribution
- Stories that are purely positive with no challenge
- Vague timelines and vague metrics
